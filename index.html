Greetings.

I am a cognitive science researcher, working at the intersection of neuroscience and machine learning. My research focuses on understanding how the human brain solves the exploration-exploitation dilemma in visual information sampling.

Currently investigating neural mechanisms of visual attention using MEG, eye-tracking, and computational modeling. In my work I challenge traditional assumptions about visual processing, for example that saccade onset, not fixation onset, best explains the timing of early cortical responses during naturalistic vision.

Labs

  • Kietzmann Lab

    We investigate principles of neural information processing using tools from machine learning and deep learning, applied to neuroimaging data recorded at high temporal (EEG/MEG) and spatial (fMRI) resolution. Headed by Prof. Tim Kietzmann.

  • Neurobiopsychology Lab

    Human embodied cognition at the intersection of neuroscience, biology, and psychology. Headed by Prof. Peter König.
